Masala rice, also known as spiced rice, is a flavorful and aromatic dish popular in many cuisines, especially in Indian and South Asian cooking. It is a combination of cooked rice and a variety of spices and ingredients that add a burst of flavor to the dish. The spices commonly used in masala rice include cumin, coriander, turmeric, and garam masala, among others. Additional ingredients like onions, garlic, ginger, and vegetables such as peas, carrots, and bell peppers are often included to enhance the taste and texture. Masala rice can be enjoyed as a standalone dish or served as a side dish with curries or grilled meats. It offers a delightful medley of flavors and is a popular choice for a hearty and satisfying meal.

Ingredients:

2 cups of rice
1 medium-sized onion, finely chopped
2 cloves of garlic, minced
1-inch piece of ginger, grated
1 medium-sized tomato, finely chopped
1 cup of mixed vegetables (e.g., peas, carrots, bell peppers)
2 teaspoons of cumin seeds
1 teaspoon of turmeric powder
1 teaspoon of coriander powder
1/2 teaspoon of garam masala
Salt to taste
3 tablespoons of cooking oil or ghee
Fresh coriander leaves for garnish (optional)


Instructions:

* Rinse the rice under cold water until the water runs clear. Soak the rice in water for about 30 minutes, then drain and set aside.

* Heat oil or ghee in a large pan or pot over medium heat. Add the cumin seeds and let them splutter.

* Add the chopped onions to the pan and sauté until they turn golden brown.

* Stir in the minced garlic and grated ginger. Sauté for a minute until fragrant.

* Add the chopped tomatoes and cook until they soften and turn mushy.

* Now, add the mixed vegetables to the pan and cook for a few minutes until they are partially cooked.

* Add the turmeric powder, coriander powder, garam masala, and salt. Mix well to coat the vegetables evenly with the spices.

* Add the soaked and drained rice to the pan and gently mix it with the vegetable-spice mixture.

* Pour in enough water (about 3.5 cups) to cook the rice. Adjust the water quantity based on the rice brand's instructions.

* Bring the mixture to a boil. Once it boils, reduce the heat to low, cover the pan with a tight-fitting lid, and let it simmer for about 15-20 minutes or until the rice is fully cooked and fluffy.

* Once the rice is cooked, turn off the heat and let it sit covered for an additional 5 minutes to allow the flavors to meld together.

* Fluff the rice gently with a fork and garnish with fresh coriander leaves if desired.


Your Masala rice is now ready to be served..! Enjoy it as a standalone dish or as a flavorful side dish with your favorite curries or grilled meats..
